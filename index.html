Capsule CRM — Spring Clean Session
Prepared by Time Assist · j.okk [at] timeassist.co.uk

Session Summary

A 30-minute spring clean session reviewing how the Opex team is using Capsule after several months of live use. The focus was on resolving specific issues (email storage errors, duplicate user accounts), improving project hygiene, and reinforcing good habits around tasks and pipeline management. The team is generally using Capsule well — the main challenges are behavioural (task completion discipline, project closure) rather than technical.

Date: April 2026 Attendee: Mirry Oakes Team: Chris, Steve, Dave, Sarah, Angela

Key Notes

Email storage error (Angela)

▶

Angela reported an “unable to store email” error when trying to save emails to Capsule via the sidebar. The issue was caused by two things: one contact (Jason) had a different email address in Capsule than the one the email was sent from, and another contact (Hydro) had no linked project — Angela was trying to store under a project that did not exist for that contact.

Fix: Store the conversation under the contact or organisation directly, and update Jason’s email address in Capsule to match the one he is actually using.

Steve’s duplicate account (saving £17/month)

▶

Steve has two Capsule accounts — his main Opex account and a separate ACS account (£17/month) solely for storing emails from his ACS email address. The ACS account has never actually been used to store any emails.

Solution: Add Steve’s ACS email address to his main Opex account, then use the Capsule mail dropbox (BCC the dropbox address when sending emails from ACS) to store those conversations without needing a separate user. The ACS account has been suspended. Once Steve confirms the dropbox method works, the ACS account can be deleted and the records allocated to his main account.

Dave’s Outlook authentication

▶

Dave’s Outlook connection is showing “Authentication failed”. This is likely caused by his recent computer change.

Fix: Go to My Preferences → Mailbox Connections → disconnect from Outlook → reconnect. This should re-authenticate the connection on the new machine.

Chris’s email connection

▶

Chris’s mailbox shows as not connected in Capsule, yet he has been storing some emails (visible in the activity log). He may be using the mail dropbox method rather than the sidebar.

Next step: Mirry to check whether Chris has the Outlook sidebar installed or is using the dropbox, and ensure his mailbox connection is set up properly.

Tracks — reinstated and improved

▶

Tracks (repeatable task sequences) were originally set up, then deleted at Chris’s request, and have now been reinstated by Mirry with improvements. The new tracks for Principal Design and Temporary Works Coordinator work are more detailed and practical. The final task in each track triggers an email to Mirry to invoice.

Limitation: If a team member accidentally completes the final task early, the invoice notification fires prematurely and cannot be undone without deleting and reapplying the track (which resets all dates). The workaround is to push the task forward by a week and check back.

Project hygiene

▶

Several projects are not on the project board, have no tags, and some have no tasks or tracks applied. Mirry needs to do a cursory review:

  • Ensure all projects are added to a board
  • Ensure all projects have a service tag
  • Close completed projects — if unsure, add a task to the project lead (Angela/Sarah) to confirm closure
  • Check for duplicate projects (e.g. Steve creating a project independently from a won opportunity)

A saved list sorted by “Added On” was created so Mirry can see recently created projects and spot any she did not know about. A filtered version excluding projects owned by Mirry shows what other team members have created.

Sales pipeline

▶

The pipeline is being used. Mirry has taken ownership of all opportunities (removed from Chris’s name to avoid confusion). Mirry also maintains a separate spreadsheet for invoicing and cash flow tracking — the pipeline is for sales/BD, the spreadsheet is for financial management.

Chris wants the pipeline to function as a cash flow view, but Capsule is not a financial tool — the two systems serve different purposes.

Task discipline

▶

Most of the team is completing tasks on time. Steve is the exception — tasks overdue from December were eventually cleared after a focused session with Mirry, but new overdue tasks are accumulating.

The team is starting Friday team meetings where Mirry will go through overdue tasks with each person as peer accountability.

How to check: Tasks → List view → Show Overdue → filter by assignee

Capsule AI

▶

AI Assist was enabled in account settings. Contact enrichment was turned off (not useful for Opex). AI summaries are only available on the Growth plan (double the current price). The Growth plan also offers automatic email storage and enhanced reporting, but neither is essential enough to justify the cost at this stage.
