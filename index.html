1

Email storage errors

▶

Angela reported an "unable to store email" error when using the Outlook sidebar. Two causes were identified:

Different email address: One contact (Jason) had a different email address in Capsule than the one the email was sent from. Capsule could not match the email to the contact. Fix: update Jason's email address in Capsule to match the one he actually uses.

No linked project: Angela tried to store an email under a project for a contact (Hydro) that had no project. Fix: store the conversation under the contact or organisation directly — you do not need to link it to a project.

When storing an email, if the "Project" option shows nothing, just click "Store Conversation" under the person or organisation instead. You only need to link to a project if one exists for that contact.
2

Steve's duplicate account

▶

Steve has two Capsule accounts — his main Opex account and a separate ACS account (£17/month) solely for storing emails from his ACS email address. The ACS account has never been used to store any emails.

The fix: Add Steve's ACS email address to his main Opex account, then use the Capsule mail dropbox (BCC the dropbox address when sending emails from ACS) to store those conversations without needing a separate user.

The ACS account has been suspended. Once Steve confirms the dropbox method works, the ACS account can be deleted and the records allocated to his main account. This saves £17/month.

How to use the mail dropbox

1
Find the dropbox address
Account Settings → Mail Dropbox → copy the dropbox email address.
2
BCC it when sending
When sending an email from the ACS account, put the dropbox address in the BCC field.
3
Email is stored automatically
The email is stored against the matching contact in Capsule based on the recipient's email address.

Tracks — reinstated and improved

▶

Tracks (repeatable task sequences) were originally set up, then deleted, and have now been reinstated by Mirry with improvements. The new tracks for Principal Design and Temporary Works Coordinator work are more detailed and practical.

Invoice notification: The final task in each track triggers an email to Mirry to raise an invoice. If a team member accidentally completes the final task early, the notification fires prematurely and cannot be undone without deleting and reapplying the entire track (which resets all dates).

Workaround: If a task is completed out of order, push the notification task forward by a week and check back. Remind the team to complete tasks in sequence.

Consolidated projects: Where a client has both Principal Design and Temporary Works, these are now under one project with separate tracks applied, rather than two separate projects. This avoids confusion from team members putting tasks on the wrong project.

If someone ticks off the final task by accident, the invoice email fires immediately. You cannot undo it — you can only push the task forward and check back. Make sure the team knows to complete tasks in order.

Task discipline

▶

Most of the team completes tasks on time. Steve is the exception — overdue tasks from December were eventually cleared after a focused session, but new ones accumulate.

Friday team meetings

Starting weekly Friday meetings where Mirry goes through overdue tasks with each person. Use the overdue task filter:

1
Go to Tasks → List view
Click "Show Overdue" at the top.
2
Filter by person
Change "Assigned to" to the team member you want to review.
3
Go through each one
Either complete it, add a note explaining why it could not be done and move it to a realistic date, or delete it if no longer relevant.
If a task cannot be completed, add a note in the additional detail field explaining why, then move it to a realistic future date. Do not leave it sitting overdue with no explanation.
